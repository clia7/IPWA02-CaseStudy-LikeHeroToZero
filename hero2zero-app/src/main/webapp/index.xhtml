Green Trace

We are non-profit organization dedicated to increasing transparency in the global fight against climate change. We believe that access to clear, reliable data on CO2 emissions is essential for accountability and progress.

Our platform provides an accessible overview of estimated annual emissions by companies and countries, helping users understand who contributes most to global emissions. All data is collected from publicly available sources, processed carefully, and presented in a way that is both informative and easy to navigate.

By making emissions data visible and understandable, Green Trace empowers individuals, researchers, and policymakers to make informed decisions and take meaningful climate action. Together, we can trace emissions — and drive change.

What is CO₂?

Carbon dioxide (CO2) is a naturally occurring gas composed of one carbon atom and two oxygen atoms. It is colorless, odorless, and exists in the Earth’s atmosphere in small concentrations. Although it is a minor component of the air we breathe, it plays a crucial role in the planet’s climate.

Where does CO2 come from?

CO₂ is released into the atmosphere through both natural and human-made processes:

Natural Sources

  • Volcanic eruptions
  • Respiration by animals and humans
  • Decomposition of organic matter
  • Ocean release

Human Activities (Anthropogenic Sources)

  • Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas)
  • Deforestation (fewer trees = more CO2)
  • Industrial processes (cement, chemicals)

Why does CO2 matter?

CO2 is a greenhouse gas, meaning it traps heat in the Earth’s atmosphere. While this is necessary to keep the planet warm enough for life, excessive CO2 from human activities has intensified the greenhouse effect, leading to global warming and climate change.

How can we reduce CO2 emissions?

  • Switching to renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro)
  • Improving energy efficiency
  • Using public transportation or cycling
  • Planting trees and protecting forests
  • Supporting carbon-neutral or low-emission products

How We Collect Data

At Green Trace, we are committed to transparency and accuracy. Our emissions data is compiled from a combination of publicly available sources, international databases, and verified third-party reports.

We collect and process data from the following key sources:

  • Governmental Reports: National environmental agencies and statistical offices provide annual CO2 emission figures.
  • Corporate Disclosures: Sustainability and environmental reports from verified corporate sources.
  • Scientific Databases: Trusted institutions like the Global Carbon Project and UNFCCC.
  • Automated Web Crawlers: Ethically scraping updates from recognized sources.

All collected data undergoes a multi-step validation process before publication. This includes cross-referencing, checking for inconsistencies, and flagging outliers. Our goal is to empower public and policymakers with clear and trustworthy data to drive real climate action.
